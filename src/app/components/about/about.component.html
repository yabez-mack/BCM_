Chhattisgarh is a state presently with 27 states. Bastar
is a south-part of Chhattisgarh, a state located centrally in India. The area
of Bastar was 40,836 kilometer square. Later it got divided into 7 districts-
Bastar, Dantewada, Kanker, Sukma, Narayanpur, Bijapur, Kondagoan.There are many
local tribes living in this specific part of India, namely- The Gond, The Halba,
The Mahara, The Thakad, The Dhurvaa, The Abhuj Maria, The Bhatra, The Telunga.  Population of Chhattisgarh is 25.5 million as
per 2011 census. The Literacy rate in Chhattisgarh state is 61% according to
census data 2011.

People in Chhattisgarh are mostly affected by the Naxals (Maoist).
There were many attack attempted by the
Maoists group. In 2007, the Maoist killed 55 policemen in Rani Bodli,
Chhatisgarh. In 2010, 76 of the Central Reserve Police Force were killed in
Dantewada. In 2013, 32 were killed including Mahendra Karma who was a political
leader and some congress leader of Chhattisgarh were killed by Maoist in Darbha
valley, Sukma dist., Chhattisgarh. In
2019, a newly elected MLA Bheema Ram Mandavi was killed by a bomb blast in Syamgiri,
Dantewada Dist., Chhattisgarh. In 30th of August 2019,
a village head was killed by the Maoist in bade Guddra,
Dantewada Dist.. And there are many other incidents happen each day.

The custom of untouchability, caste system, human sacrifice including
concieved women are there in practice.
These people believe that their Gods are the nature and Idols,
they also worship spirits and demons.
In 2015, more than 50 villages in Bastar region banned Christianity.
Open air preaching, Evangelism in these villages are difficult and villagers
who believe in Christ are persecuted.

At first in 1987, Late. Rev. S. Kasirajan from Tamil Nadu State came alone to Bastar region
along with his wife Mani Megalai and he was the first missionary to start open
air preaching in villages. His vision was to turn Bastar to Christ.
Then on 1993, Rev. J. Ravi Kumar Silva came and joined the ministry.
Bastar For Christ Missionary Movement was founded by Late. Rev. S. Kasirajan in 1993
and was reregistered in 2005.The first church was built in Dantewada on 1993 with 30
believers. The persecution was still going on as the villagers didnâ€™t permitted
them to get their share of drinking water and renting cycle for ministry. In
the mist of these troubles they built many churches and appointed many pastors
under them.
In 2003, they built their first hostel in Balood village with 15 children.
All the children were from single parent and some of them were affected from maoist.

They struggled a lot for the growth of the minstry and on 5th of November 2011,
Rev. S. Kasirajan buried his dear wife Mani Megalai and completing 4 years after her death
he completed his journey and was buried beside his wife.

- Present Status - After the death of the founder, Presently the organization is directed by Rev. J. Ravi Kumar Silva and with his wife R. Staline Milkal Silva. Inspite of all challenges, they are continuing the vision of the founder of the ministry. The mission was only in Bastar Dist. But now it has been spread in whole south of Chhattisgarh state. Currently the organization has 60 pastors, 35 churches and 2 hostels with 80 children and 9 staffs. Church is being planted on every place where there is no church.
